# Reflection Guide -- Quick Reference

This is a condensed version of docs/reflection.md. Use this to decide WHEN to trigger reflection and WHAT questions to ask at each moment.

---

## The 7 Question Principles

These are the core moves that make reflection stick. Use them everywhere:

1. **Ask "who specifically?"** -- Never accept "users." Push for named people, real scenarios.
2. **Ask "what happens today?"** -- If nobody has this problem badly enough to have a workaround, the problem might not be real.
3. **Ask "what would kill this?"** -- Force the builder to name their assumptions and conditions that would prove them wrong.
4. **Ask "if you could only build ONE thing?"** -- Scope clarity comes from constraints, not brainstorming.
5. **Ask "how will you know?"** -- Every build needs a measurable outcome. No metric = no learning.
6. **Challenge politely, not passively.** -- If the builder says "everyone needs this," push back: "Can you name 3 people who told you they need it?"
7. **Match intensity to context.** -- Vague ideas need deep probing. Builders tracking habits on paper for 6 months need scope discipline, not problem discovery.

## When to Trigger Reflection

| Trigger | What to ask | Phase |
|---------|------------|-------|
| **New idea, vague description** | "Who specifically has this problem? Can you name 3 real people?" | Shape (Problem validation) |
| **Scope is growing beyond pitch** | "This wasn't in the pitch. Is it essential or scope creep?" | Build |
| **Ticket taking 3x longer than expected** | "Is this critical path or should we stub it?" | Build |
| **Builder hasn't engaged in 3+ days** | "Still excited? What's blocking you?" | Any phase |
| **Key assumption from Shape invalidated** | "Your pitch assumed X. That's not holding up. Reshape?" | Build or Learn |
| **Feature request mid-build** | "How does this serve the core job? Can it wait for cycle 2?" | Build |
| **Post-deploy, no users after 1 week** | "Is this distribution or product?" | Learn |
| **Builder says "everyone needs this"** | "Can you name 3 people who actually told you?" | Shape |
| **Builder overthinking** | "You're not going to think your way to certainty. Ship it." | Any phase |
| **Scope creeping during planning** | "Every feature you add is a question you're asking users. How many can they handle?" | Shape or Build |

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

What NOT to do:

- **Don't interrogate.** 3-5 questions per pause, not 10. Give the builder room to think.
- **Don't ask questions you can answer.** If the competitor data is available, research it first, then ask: "I found 3 competitors -- none target parents. Does that change your thinking?"
- **Don't front-load all reflection.** Shape shouldn't feel like filling out a form. Spread the questions.
- **Don't block on unanswered questions.** If the builder doesn't know, note it and move on. Revisit when there's more signal.
- **Don't assume.** "I'm assuming you got 50 visits..." is fake data. Say "I don't have data on this" instead.

## Key Moments for Deep One-Liners

These land best at transitions. Use sparingly, only when earned:

- After killing an idea: "Killing an idea early isn't failure. It's the cheapest lesson you'll ever get."
- After painful scope cut: "The hardest part of building isn't writing code. It's deciding what not to write."
- After first real user: "One real user who comes back is worth more than a thousand who sign up."
- When overthinking: "You're not going to think your way to certainty. Ship it and let the users tell you what's missing."
- When scope creeps: "Every feature you add is a question you're asking your user to answer. How many questions can they handle?"
- After no users: "No users isn't a failure signal. It's a distribution signal. The product might be fine -- the megaphone might be broken."
- When stuck: "Sometimes the hardest part is just opening the laptop again."
- Starting cycle 2: "Cycle 1 was a guess. Cycle 2 is a conversation with your users. That's when it gets interesting."

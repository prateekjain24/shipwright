# Go-to-Market Playbook for Solo Builders

Getting your first 10 users is not a marketing problem. It's a distribution problem. You don't need a growth strategy. You need courage and a list of people to talk to.

This is scrappy. This is for side projects. Not enterprise launches.

---

## The Mindset

You have something real to show. Don't hide it. Don't wait for it to be perfect. Every day you wait is a day you don't have feedback.

Your job for week 1: share this with 10 people who might care, get honest feedback, iterate.

---

## 1. Write Your One-Liner

This is the sentence that explains what you built and for whom. One sentence. No jargon.

**Formula:** "{What you built} for {who needs it}"

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "A habit tracking application with ML-powered insights" | "Habit tracker for busy parents" |
| "A decentralized social platform leveraging web3 principles" | "Tweeting, just for your friends" |
| "An AI-powered productivity suite optimized for remote teams" | "Weekly planner for freelancers" |

**Why this works:** It's specific. It tells someone in 5 seconds whether this is for them.

---

## 2. Write Your Tweet

280 characters. Written like a person, not a brand. Conversational. Include a link.

**Formula:** A sentence or two about the problem, then the link. No hype.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Excited to announce the launch of our innovative habit-tracking solution. Leveraging cutting-edge technology to optimize behavioral change." | "Built a habit tracker for parents. No algorithms, no gamification -- just honest tracking. Let me know what you think: {link}" |
| "HUGE NEWS! We've just shipped the future of {industry}. Game changer incoming!" | "My parents were struggling to get my kids to brush teeth consistently. Built this habit tracker to help. Still very beta: {link}" |

**Why this works:** It's humble. It's specific. It sounds like a real person. People share things that sound real.

---

## 3. Write Your 3-Sentence Pitch

A short pitch for sharing in communities (Reddit, Discord, Slack groups, etc.). Not a tweet. More context than a one-liner, but still short.

**Formula:** Problem (one sentence) + Solution (one sentence) + Ask (one sentence)

**Example:**
> "My parents were struggling to get my kids to stick to daily routines. I built a simple habit tracker that lets them set a goal for each kid and check off daily progress. I'd love honest feedback on what's missing."

**Bad version:**
> "We've built a comprehensive behavioral management platform that leverages gamification theory and social dynamics to drive sustained habit formation. Our solution integrates with existing family management systems and provides advanced analytics. Visit our platform today."

**Why this works:** It's human. It admits you built this for a real problem. It asks for help instead of demanding interest.

---

## 4. First-10 Users Plan

Don't overthink this. List the 10 (or 5, or 15) actual people or communities where you'll share this. Be specific. Not "Twitter" -- which Twitter communities.

### Where to Share (Pick 3-5)

| Channel | How | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| **Reddit** | Find a relevant subreddit, post in "show and tell" | r/parenting: "Built a habit tracker for kids -- would love your feedback" |
| **Twitter** | Tweet thread, ask for feedback, engage with replies | @yourhandle: "launched habit tracker...would appreciate RT" |
| **Communities** | Discord servers, Slack communities you're in | Indie Hackers Slack: share in #launches |
| **Communities** | Facebook groups (if relevant) | Parenting groups, productivity groups |
| **HN/Indie Hackers** | Tell HN post or "Show IH" | "Show IH: Built a habit tracker for parents" |
| **Cold email** | DM friends, ex-colleagues, people in your network | "Hey Sarah, you mentioned needing a habit tracker... I built this" |

### People to DM (Name names)

Don't just say "email my network." Actually list people:
- Sarah (my manager at last job -- she has 3 kids)
- Tom (met him at XOXO, he blogs about parenting)
- Ava (college friend, now a UX designer, I want her feedback)

Personalized is better. Send them a 2-3 sentence message:
> "Hey Sarah, I built a habit tracker for parents that tracks routines instead of just goals. Would love for you to try it and tell me what's broken. Link: {url}"

## 5. Launch Day Checklist

Day 1 is when you have the most energy. Use it. Don't spread it out.

### Before You Launch
- [ ] Deploy URL is live and tested
- [ ] Analytics is working (load your site, check Plausible dashboard)
- [ ] Tweet is written and ready to go
- [ ] First-10 list is filled in with names
- [ ] You've written personal DMs for the 5 people closest to the problem

### Morning Of
- [ ] Post to one community (Reddit or Indie Hackers)
- [ ] Tweet (with link, be specific, ask for feedback)
- [ ] DM your 5 closest people (personalized, 2-3 sentences)
- [ ] Share in one Slack/Discord community you're in

### That Afternoon
- [ ] Check for replies on Reddit, Twitter, communities
- [ ] Reply to people (thank them, ask follow-up questions)
- [ ] Update SHIPWRIGHT.md with links to posts/threads

### That Week
- [ ] Wait for feedback, iterate quickly
- [ ] Track which channel brought the first 3 users (that's your distribution channel)
- [ ] Fix obvious bugs people report
- [ ] Don't add features yet -- just listen

---

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't wait for it to be perfect.** It's not. Ship it anyway.
- **Don't spam.** You're not selling. You're asking for feedback from people who might care.
- **Don't copy-paste.** Every message to every community should be slightly different, personalized to that community.
- **Don't post and disappear.** You need to be present for the first 24 hours. Reply to comments. Answer questions. Show you care.
- **Don't ask "anyone interested in X?"** Be specific. "I built X, it does Y, here's why I think you'd care."
- **Don't hide behind the product.** "Hi, I'm the person who built this" is more compelling than "Hey everyone, check this out."

---

## After Week 1: What Success Looks Like

- You got 3-10 real people to try it (not just followers, actual usage)
- You have 1-2 pieces of real feedback (not "cool" -- actual feedback like "I wanted X to do Y")
- You know which distribution channel works best (Twitter vs Reddit vs community vs email)
- You have a list of bugs to fix or features people asked for

You don't need viral. You don't need 1000 signups. You need 10 people who used it and came back.

---

## The Philosophy

Distribution for side projects is simple:
1. You care about this problem
2. Other people probably care too
3. Tell them it exists
4. Listen to what they think
5. Make it better
6. Repeat

No growth hacks. No influencer lists. Just honest sharing with people who might actually benefit.
